Model. Linear membrane (tension only, no bending): N∇²w = −σg. Truth = plane-stress FEM with corner loads and Miller–Hedgepeth wrinkling, sag pinned at the corner tabs. Reference = uniform-tension drumhead with N = F⁄(√2·L), w꜀ = pL²⁄4πN, σ_slope = pR_m⁄2√2N, R_m = L⁄√π.
Why they differ. No uniform tension field can exactly balance four corner point loads; the FEM interior carries ≈1.55× the nominal N (wrinkled edges shed load inward), so the real film sits somewhat flatter than the ideal-uniform reference at the same pull — its free edges scallop instead.
Scope. v1 is the gravity billow only — solar pressure (~9 µPa), thermal wrinkling and boom buckling are out of scope (hooks in source). Small-slope regime; readouts flag when it breaks.
